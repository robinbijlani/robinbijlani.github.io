Work

  • Exotics database website (in progress)

    back end development (PHP/Laravel)

    The Exotics database website is being created for the University of Florida and Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission to share their invasive reptile captures and sightings. Records include measurements, necropsy results, tracking data, etc. The website allows privileged users to view this data and filter it via custom queries. Maps are also generated from each query.

  • EIRAMP Tablet app

    mobile app development (PhoneGap)

    The University of Florida's Everglades Invasive Reptile and Amphibian Monitoring Program (EIRAMP) involves driving along standard routes in the Everglades while identifying all the native and nonnative reptiles, amphibians and mammals found. The EIRAMP tablet app facilitates data recording and validates the entered data, greatly reducing human error as well as data entry and proofing time.

  • Don't Let It Loose bookmark

    graphic design

    This bookmark created for the Everglades Cooperative Invasive Species Management Area (ECISMA) lists surprising facts about invasive species in Florida and reminds people not to let their pets loose.

  • IveGot1 bumper sticker

    graphic design

    These promotional bumper stickers were created to raise awareness about the IveGot1 website, which allows anyone to report a sighting of a nonnative animal or plant.

  • Triangulate Android app (PhoneGap)

    mobile app development

    Triangulate is an Android app built to allow field biologists to accurately estimate the location of tracked animals using triangulation.

  • The Croc Docs website

    web design development

    The website for the University of Florida's "Croc Docs" provides information about a team of biologists, ecological modelers, and outreach specialists in south Florida that focuses on crocodilians, invasive reptiles, threatened and endangered species, and climate change. The site also allows visitors to view the location history of crocodilians fitted with radiotransmitters through an interactive map.

    Visit crocdoc.ifas.ufl.edu
